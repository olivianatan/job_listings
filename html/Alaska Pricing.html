## ALASKA AIRLINES' STORY

Alaska Airlines is one of the most respected names in aviation and flies throughout its namesake state and the Lower 48, as well as to Hawaii, Canada, Mexico and Costa Rica. Our roots date to 1932 and are symbolized by the Alaska Native painted on the tails of our aircraft. Guided by what we call the "Alaska Spirit", we pride ourselves on providing a lifeline to remote communities while delivering renowned service to everyone we fly. This commitment has brought us national and international recognition. We've been honored with a variety of awards by readers of Travel + Leisure, Conde Nast Traveler, USA Today and others. Alaska, with Virgin America, is the premier airline for people on the West Coast, and together with its sister carrier Horizon Air, flies to more than 115 destinations. The two airlines are subsidiaries of Alaska Air Group Inc. (NYSE: ALK) with annual revenues exceeding $7 billion.

## POSITION INFORMATION

Role Summary
The Senior Pricing Analyst analyzes changes in market conditions such as capacity, competition, schedules, and fares to develop, implement and track appropriate pricing strategies.

Scope & Complexity

  • This individual contributor role supports pricing analysis activities for Alaska Air Group (AAG) and its subsidiaries.
Key Duties

  • Analyzes changes in market conditions such as capacity, competition, schedules, and fares to develop, implement, and track appropriate pricing strategies.
  • Reviews competitor fare changes in assigned markets/regions and implements changes based on strategies.
  • Leverages subject matter expertise to manage a more challenging scope of markets in terms of fare activity and competitive landscape and uses this ability to help train new analysts.
  • Develops, implements, and assesses complex strategies with little supervisory guidance.
  • Proactively develops and communicates market and pricing strategies to the leadership team and teammates.
  • Takes initiative and effectively communicates with Inventory, Marketing, Sales, Legal, and other stakeholders and understands the implications of actions and reactions.
  • Monitors and analyzes market data to achieve/exceed PRASM goals.
  • Leverages expertise with pricing tools, databases, and processes.
  • Owns and fulfills assigned collateral duty and ad-hoc projects.
  • Analyzes large business questions by manipulating data sources to create actionable analysis and/or reports.
  • Monitors KPI performance and adjusts and communicates strategies needed to meet goals.
  • Assists in implementing improvements to processes and reports.
  • Represents Pricing's perspective at meetings and on projects with other departments and external vendors.
  • Performs complex ad hoc projects and reports.
  • Supports new hire training, mentors new hires, and helps to improve their analytical and technical skills.
  • Maintains, develops, and/or implements effective system enhancements.
  • Performs other duties as assigned.
Job-Specific Skills
Required

  • A minimum of 2 years of pricing/revenue management experience or 3 years of experience in an analytical role; will waive one year of analytical experience with a Master's degree.
  • A Bachelor of Arts or a Bachelor of Science degree, with a focus in business administration, economics, statistics, mathematics, industrial engineering or a related discipline, is required.
  • Proficiency with Microsoft Office applications (e.g., Word, PowerPoint, and Outlook), with advanced skills in Excel.
  • Expert with data analysis programs (e.g., Access, Business Objects, SQL, or Brio).
  • Creative problem-solver, willing and able to work on multiple tasks, take calculated risks, and assume responsibility for results in an extremely fast paced environment.
  • Ability to extract, interpret, and present large quantities of data in a clear and concise manner.
  • Excellent verbal, written, and analytical skills.
  • High school diploma or equivalent.
  • Minimum age of 18.
  • Must be authorized to work in the U.S.
Preferred

  • Prior airline experience in revenue management, revenue analysis, pricing, or planning.
Job-Specific Leadership Expectations

  • Embody our values to own safety, do the right thing, be kind-hearted, deliver performance, and be remarkable.
