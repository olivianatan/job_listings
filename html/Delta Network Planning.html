Network Planning Sr Analyst

United States, Georgia, Atlanta

Network Planning

06-Jun-2022

Ref #: 15057

How you'll help us Keep Climbing (overview & key responsibilities)

The Network Planning Sr Analyst position is responsible for coordinating the design and development of Delta's flight network schedule. This includes coordinating with the Scheduling team to understand market frequency requirements while working in the confines of limited fleet and station resources.

Responsibilities include:

  • Reviewing connectivity of the banks, pattern review, curfew and slot compliance, gating hubs and outstations and overnight maintenance compliance.

  • Successful candidate should be able to identify opportunities and omissions in the schedule and recommend courses of action.

  • This position requires attention to detail and quick decision-making skills to optimize the airline schedule.

  • Critical thinking skills- understanding what trade-offs to make and when to make them is key to being successful in the role.

  • Working cross functionally across multiple groups in Network and Revenue Management.

What you need to succeed (minimum qualifications)

  • The candidate should have strong analytical and computer skills, as well as airline experience and knowledge of the industry.

  • Must have strong business/management skills and excellent verbal and written communication skills.

  • Knowledge of Microsoft Word, Powerpoint, and Excel is required.

  • Must have the ability to work well with others in a time sensitive and team environment with minimal supervision, be results oriented and capable of meeting or exceeding deadlines with attention to detail and follow through.

  • Where permitted by applicable law, must have received or be willing to receive the COVID-19 vaccine by date of hire to be considered for U.S.-based job, if not currently employed by Delta Air Lines, Inc.

What will give you a competitive edge (preferred qualifications)

  • College degree or equivalent work experience preferred. Prefer major in Industrial Engineering, Finance, Statistics, Economics, Business, or Management.
